My Story

I'm a Black-British designer based in London, the city of double-decker buses, red phone booths, royal guards and black cabbies. I have a background in journalism, which opened me up to explore the possibilities of digital design, storytelling and visual communication

My origin story of how I got into UI/UX started in the winter of 2020, I was going through a quarter life crisis, being a journalism graduate that didn't want to go into that industry. I knew I needed a career to match my inquistiveness, and interest in creative technology. After a few angsty Google searches, I stumbled upon a model by Paul Sohn, which is based on four intersections to help identify your career sweet spot. Additionally, a book called Ikigai, based on a Japanese proverb, led me to take into careful consideration my strengths, what I can contribute to the world, my passions and my competencies.

Once I gathered all of these components I was left with a clearer idea of the type of career I wanted: A career which is both mentally stimulating and challenging, allows me to dissect and pick people's brain's, allows room for collobrative and creative outlets, focuses on uncovering the root of a problem and providing solutions. In summary I was looking for a career that married together creativity, my desire to understand people and technology

So when the end of my research lead me to UI/UX Design, I strongly resonated with this career as all of my previous experiences and interests tied into it. It is an industry where I can amalgamate my interests and skills in technology, research, user psychology and creativity. My desire to understand people stories and convey them has not changed; through user-centered research and design I can help people navigate their relationship with technology and the thought of that excites me!
