Introduction

Overview

In this project, I present “Travel Buddy” a responsive web application which focuses on solving some of the major problems travel seekers face by integrating a personalisation approach to help users, save time, money and created personalised travel experiences.

As, a UX designer my primary focus is on user satisfaction and ensuring that the product fulfils the user’s needs. Therefore I enlisted the use of a product development process that is user centred and solution orientated: Peter Rose’s Design thinking process

The Context

A responsive web application which focuses on solving some major problems travel seekers face by integrating a personalisation approach to help users, save time, money and created personalised travel experiences.

The Problem

Users feel that travel apps don’t do enough to meet their specific desires and needs

The Objective

To provide a solution to this problem, I designed an app that collates users data to create tailored suggestion that meet their needs

The Design Process

The Design Thinking Process

Design thinking is a non-linear, iterative process that teams use to understand users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems and create innovative solutions to prototype and test.

Competitor Analysis

In this beginning stage of the Design Thinking process I focused on gathering preliminary research to get to know this industry, get to know competitors and similar or comparable products. This is to ensure I understand what I am creating but also the landscape I am creating in.

As this is a location-based app, I conducted an analysis on two companies that I consider to be leading industry examples. I approached this by identifying the patterns that were present in both.

In this next stage, I spent my time getting to know the users and the problems that they face as it pertains to travelling. I found this empathizing phase to be invaluable; the conversations I had with different users broaden my perspective as a researcher. Many of the paint points mentioned in discussions directly inspired the development of features included in the app.

Collated Results

Affinity Mapping

In this part of the process I was able to compartmentalise certain patterns and particular pain points that were repeated or empathised. This made creating the framework to inform my design decisions water-tight, as it was backed by research.

After gathering all the findings from the Empathise stage and synthesizing the user research, I formulated a Problem statement (POV), which outlined the issues or challenges that I sought to address. The data collect enable me to create user personas, which were an archetypal representation of the users needs, goals and frustrations. This helped me focus on their needs and pain points throughout the design process.

Problem statement :

Users tend to feel overwhelmed with the deluge of options and suggestions that are based on trends and popularity, not on individual preferences. They find it difficult to find a service that is focused on meeting their specific needs.
