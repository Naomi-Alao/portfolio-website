The Process

As, a UX designer my primary focus is on user satisfaction and ensuring that the product fulfils the user’s needs. Therefore I enlisted the use of a product development process that is user centred and solution orientated: Peter Rose’s Design thinking process.

The Context

The Problem

Users feel that travel apps don’t do enough to meet their specific desires and needs

The objective

To provide a solution to this problem, I designed an app that collates users data to create tailored suggestion that meet their needs

In this beginning stage of the Design Thinking process I focused on gathering preliminary research to get to know this industry, get to know competitors and similar or comparable products. This is to ensure I understand what I am creating but also the landscape I am creating in.

As this is a location-based app, I conducted an analysis on two companies that I consider to be leading industry examples. I approached this by identifying the patterns that were present in both.

In this next stage, I spent my time getting to know the users and the problems that they face as it pertains to travelling. I found this empathizing phase to be invaluable; the conversations I had with different users broaden my perspective as a researcher. Many of the pain points mentioned in discussions directly inspired the development of features included in the app.

collated results

After gathering all the findings from the Empathise stage and synthesizing the user research, I formulated a Problem statement (POV), which will outline the issues or challenges that I’ll seek to address. The data collect enable to create user personas which were a archetypal representation of the users needs, goals and frustrations. This will help me focus on their needs and pain points throughout the design process.
