Overview

For my first project I was tasked with designing the UI of a vintage clothing sales app. This included design 2-3 key functionalities and abiding by the design requirements listed in the brief. The target audience are users between the ages of 25- 34.

The project brief explains that the founders want their vintage clothing app to include three key functionalities: create a profile, upload items to sell, leave reviews for other sellers.

They also want a competitor analysis conducted and to define a further 2 to 3 functionalities based on my research. Lastly, all deliverables will need to be ready to hand off to the developers who will implement the designs.

Challenge

Solution

In accordance with the project deliverables and based on further research, I will design he UI for the mobile version of a vintage clothing app. It will maintain several key functionalities while making the UI design ready to be handed off to developers.

Competitor Analysis

I started off this project by studying three popular vintage clothing apps: Vestaire Collective, Depop and Asos.

Analysing each of these apps I identified design principles and heuristics used within the features that I drew inspiration from for Elysian.

By analyzing competitor apps, I took note of each app's unique features and their UI patterns, which helped me in my next step of defining key functionalities and creating a user flow.

Branding description

Actualise your short term goals with Target Arrange your money however you'd like. Saving for a car? Put a target on it. Want to compartmentalise your expenses? Put a Target on it.

Use Targets for anything you'd like – from holidays to groceries or anything in between. Stay in control by keeping money for important things separate from the rest of your balance.

Aim, point and hit your Target now.

Guideing Principles

Actualisation

Without a plan you plan to fail. Set long-term and short-terms goals that turn wishes into attainable goals.

Simplicicty

Aims to be simple and easy to use interface. for both tech-savvy users to the Luddite users.

Innovative

Round up your spare change and earn up to 1.81% (AER/Gross fixed) interest on your savings.

Security

Keep Your Money Safe. Cutting-edge technology, FSCS protection and 24/7 support if you need us urgently.

Brading Guidelines

If you, like me was thrown into the adulting world ill-equipped about finances, scratched your head at terms like IRA, ROI, ISA, FICO Score, IPO, APR, Bear Market – Then Target is the app for you. Target is for people who don’t want to loiter around in a branch foyer trying to work out if the person in front could be any slower. Target is here to dispel the intimidating and complex side of finance, so that you, the user can save money quickly in preparation for a big purchase or expenditure.

Become a money detective with Target’s Trends. Trends are designed to help you discover more about your money patterns. The better you understand your money, the more it’ll do for you. Users will be able to see a graph that shows their monthly spending broken down by week, alongside a rolling average shown by a dashed line.

A solution to meet your financial goals is the Target feature. A Target is a space in your account where you can separate out your money from your main balance. They're useful if you're saving for a holiday or something special. You can stash money away so that you don't spend it from your main balance, kind of like a piggy bank.

The app will be clean yet vibrant and dynamic. That will use bursts of the primary colour, red- to evoke urgency, alertness and engagement. Alongside bright colours, bold imagery and iconography will be used to make the interface personable and easy to navigate, this is specifically to dispel the negative and intimidating rep that finance carries.

The writing style will use the language our audience uses, to make the technical stuff as clear as possible. It’ll be informal and friendly in approach. The text of choice is Merriweather Sans, a low-contrast semi-condensed sans-serif text typeface family designed to be pleasant to read at very small sizes.

The logo embodies the character of the app, in being that it aims for users to actualise their short-term goals with set targets. So, the arrow hitting the bullseye, is fitting to symbolise and motivate users to aim (draw), plan (steady) and meet (strike) their financial goals.
