About

Global Citizen is a responsive language learning app. Something unique to this project was that I developed this app for both IOS and Android operating systems. In this experience I heavily referenced Apple’s Human Interactive Guidelines and Google’s Material Design. This two-pronged approach helped me develop a keen awareness of the nuances in design systems, typefaces, functionality of both operating systems.

Problem

I designed an app to solve problems users face while learning a new language. After some research, problems users face include:

  • Lack of discipline
  • Not having enough time
  • The lack of options to practice a new language with native speakers
  • Lack of follow through. User don’t become fluent in a new language because they can’t test their language skills.

    • Solution

    In the market, there are many similar apps, Global Citizen will stand out because it will merge together many of the unique features that are seen across language apps out in the market. By doing this, users will have access to all the best learning features and methods:

  • Language courses
  • Educational games
  • Q&A chats
  • Flashcards.

    Operating Systems: IOS and Android

    The benefits of using a design system is that it is a collection of pre-built, reusable assets—components, patterns, guidance, and code—that allows its designers to build consistent digital experiences faster.

    I used Material Design 3 and Human Interface Design, respectively. Both systems have their own specific nuances, such as their navigation. HIG uses Tab bars whereas MD uses app bars and bottom navigation. Similarly, with the typefaces, IOS devices commonly use SF Pro and Android uses Roboto.
